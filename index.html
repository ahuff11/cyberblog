When Will Quantum Computing change Cryptography?

Quantum Computing, Cryptography, Cybersecurity

Quantum computing stands on the cusp of transforming cryptography and cybersecurity, introducing a shift that challenges the fundamental principles of secure information transmission. In very basic terms, quantum computers use qubits to allow them to process information in parallel, rather than the traditional binary limits of classical computers.Major companies such as IBM and Google have made great strides in achieving quantum use for specific use cases, however the application of quantum computing for cryptographic attacks struggle overcoming technical challenges such as error correction and scalability. The timeline for everyone owning a quantum computer is unknown but definitely coming in the near future. Researchers at the National INstitute of Standards and Technology are actively developing new quantum, cryptographic algorithms to counteract these new quantum threats.As the quantum era inches closer, the urgency to adopt quantum-resistant cryptographic methods becomes paramount, prompting a transition in the cybersecurity landscape.

The Future of AI and Cybersecurity

AI Defense, AI Threats, Cybersecurity

With the further and further advancement of Artificial Intelligence, the cybersecurity landscape will change with a new wave of proactive defense and adaptive resilience. AI stands as a great resource for stopping malicious actors. LLMs (Large Language Models) can be specially trained in cybersecurity attacks and patterns, thus allowing AI to detect and respond with extreme speeds. Automated threat detection and mitigation will help analysts focus their resources on more complex tasks. As long as the LLMs are updated, AI can continuously learn to stay ahead of emerging threats. Although AI is a great defense for cyberattacks it can also be used in the opposite. LLMs can be trained in zero days and attacking vulnerabilities making large scale and ongoing attacks very plausible. AI can also speed up and enhance a phishing campaign against a company. As AI is a great resource for Human kind, it can also be a great danger. It seems the future of cybersecurity could be determined on who can use AI most efficiently and effectively.
